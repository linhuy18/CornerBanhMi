Our story

Banh Mi, an iconic sandwich to the Vietnamese culture as hamburgers are to America. A hybrid of French-Vietnamese food that consists of warm crusty bread, a protein and accompanied by homemade mayo, cucumber, crunchy pickled daikon and carrot, hot chilies and topped with refreshing cucumber and herbs! Who doesn’t love a good sandwich?

Cuong Luong, the founder of Corner Banh Mi, grew up with his father's banh mi stand in their Saigon neighborhood. He learned how to bake baguettes from his father, and experimented with different flavor profiles by preparing his own meat and vegetables. The timing, proportion, and chemistry of banh mi’s individual elements made sense to him with the help of his father. Ever since his father closed up the shop in Saigon in the 1970s after the war to come to America, he would always think back to his father's banh mi. After their reunion in Chicago back in 2004, the family decided to start a banh mi shop in the Vietnamese street of Argyle. After the passing of his father, Cuong had continued his father's legacy and making the best banh mi in Chicago. In honor of his father and their success, they opened another shop in Evanston in 2009. Over the years, Corner Banh Mi started to offer more than just delicious banh mi, they now offers the best Vietnamese sweets, Che, and Vietnamese coffee and drinks.

During the French colonization, Cuong's father learned from the French themselves on how to make the best baguettes. To this day, they used the same recipe in both the stores in Chicago. The homemade dough is proofed on site, then scored with a knife to give each loaf that trademark split down the middle. Each giant rack holds 450 small loaves and they are placed into giant custom ovens all at once, set onto a track across the top of the oven. Once inside, they bake at 400 degrees for about 20 minutes, constantly spinning on the giant racks to aerate and bake each loaf evenly.
